## How to Add These to Your Document

### Format Example:

**Before:**
```markdown
55. Fielding, R.T. (2000) *Architectural Styles and the Design of Network-based Software Architectures*. Doctoral dissertation. University of California, Irvine.
```

**After:**
```markdown
55. Fielding, R.T. (2000) *Architectural Styles and the Design of Network-based Software Architectures*. Doctoral dissertation. University of California, Irvine. Available at: https://www.ics.uci.edu/~fielding/pubs/dissertation/top.htm (Accessed: 6 November 2025).
```

## Tools for Finding Papers

1. **Google Scholar**: https://scholar.google.com/
   - Search by title
   - Click "All versions" to find free PDFs
   - Look for [PDF] links on the right

2. **Semantic Scholar**: https://www.semanticscholar.org/
   - AI-powered search
   - Often shows open access versions

3. **ResearchGate**: https://www.researchgate.net/
   - Authors often upload their papers
   - Can request full text from authors

4. **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/
   - Preprint server
   - Computer Science papers

5. **CORE**: https://core.ac.uk/
   - Aggregates open access research papers

## Special Cases

### Books Without Direct Links
For books (like those by O'Reilly, Addison-Wesley, Manning), provide:
- Publisher website
- ISBN
- Note: "Available at major retailers"

Example:
```markdown
102. Martin, R.C. (2008) *Clean Code: A Handbook of Agile Software Craftsmanship*. Upper Saddle River: Prentice Hall. ISBN: 978-0132350884. Available at: https://www.pearson.com/ and major retailers.
```

### Papers Requiring Subscription
If you can only find paywalled versions:
```markdown
156. Sandhu, R.S., Coyne, E.J., Feinstein, H.L. and Youman, C.E. (1996) 'Role-based access control models', *Computer*, 29(2), pp. 38-47. doi: 10.1109/2.485845. Available at: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/485845 (Accessed: 6 November 2025) [Requires IEEE subscription].
```
